Scope

Shayofunmi Care Agency is a social and health care agency with an interest in providing Mental Health Care and Social Care support and rehabilitation of people living with mental health problems, substance use disorders, physical and learning disabilities, palliative care, etc

Our Practices

Our Practice is a fully integrated and multi-disciplinary and biopsychosocial approach, integrating all professionals involved in Mental Health and Social Care practice.

Our Team

Our team includes psychiatrists, Clinical Psychologists, social workers, occupational therapists, psychiatric nurses, counselors, carers, cooks, cleaners, and security men.

About Us

Shayofunmi Care Agency is a non-medical social and healthcare rehabilitation agency, registered and certified social care provider, located at the boundary of Ereko and Magbon town,Ikorodu, Ogun State. The agency was founded in 2016, by a team of social and health care professionals. We have since then developed into a household agency in both Ogun and Lagos States by providing social care rehabilitation services for people living with mental health challenges, substance use disorders as well as physical and medical health challenges.

The agency has provided services for people living with different kinds of difficulties such as mental health, drug abuse, learning and physical disabilities, palliative care and all other medical conditions both within Ogun and Lagos States, Nigeria. At Shayofunmi Care Agency, our idea is that every individual has tremendous potential to function in our society irrespective of their social adversities. This informs our passion for providing comprehensive and integrated social care rehabilitation services to enable children, adolescents and adults live meaningful life and function well in our society.

An Administrative Officer

An Administrative Officer is responsible for providing administrative support to an organization, managing the daily tasks of the organization by providing administrative and clerical support. They work closely with upper management to review organization operations and look for ways to maximize internal processes.

Psychiatrist

A psychiatrist is a medical doctor who specializes in mental health. A psychiatrist understands both physical and mental health conditions, and they will consider the links between them. Psychiatrists must train at medical school before training in psychiatry. A psychiatrist can diagnose and prescribe medication to treat a variety of complex mental illnesses, such as: • schizophrenia • depression • borderline personality disorder • bipolar disorder • Substance abuse

Nurse

Nurse is a person who cares for the sick. He/she is a licensed health care professional who practices independently or is supervised by a psychiatrist, physician, surgeon who is skilled in promoting and maintaining health.

Social Worker

Social worker is a practice-based profession that concerns itself with individuals, families, groups, communities and society as a whole in an effort to meet basic needs and enhance social functioning, collective responsibility and overall well being.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some questions we get frequently.

  • What does Mental Illness means?

    The term mental illness refers to a group of biological brain disorders that affect a person's perception of reality, their thinking, and how they cope with their daily lives. A number of disorders are common, including anxiety, depression, schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, etc. Adolescents and young adults are particularly vulnerable, though people of all ages are susceptible.

  • My family has no history of mental illness. Does that mean I am immune?

    It is true that family history plays an influential role in the development of mental illness, but it is not the only one. Psychosocial and environmental factors contribute to risk factors. No one is immune to developing mental health illnesses.

  • Is the rehabilitation program the same for all patients?

    Your intervention is tailored to the challenges or medical issues that arise in the initial and subsequent assessments. Patient diagnoses and abilities are unique, so it's imperative to remember that every patient is unique. No rehabilitation program is the same.
    It is our goal to work with you and your family to ensure the best possible outcome, but you are the most important member of the team and your commitment to your rehabilitation is what makes the difference. You will be expected to practice the techniques you are taught during your daily therapy sessions so that your team can offer you support and guidance so that you can become confident and independent in these activities.

  • How often do I receive Therapy?

    Every patient has individual therapeutic needs, so your therapy "mix" and focus may be different from those of your fellow patients. Your diagnostic category will also determine the intensity of the therapy you receive. Our patients receive the daily intervention (based on therapeutic need) five days a week throughout their stay, with a reduced program on Saturday mornings and most public holidays. Therapy doesn’t end in the gym, however: patients are expected to carry their newly learnt skills over into the ward environment under the guidance and assistance of the rehabilitation nursing team.

  • How do you ensure patient satisfaction?

    The Rehabilitation team is dedicated to providing our patients with quality services that meet and exceed their expectations. Providing constructive feedback from you and your family is the only way we can accomplish this. As you progress through your stay and upon discharge, you will be asked to provide feedback. Here are some resources we encourage you to use: The customer comment cards available at the unit Ad hoc meetings with staff The patient survey on discharge (email or SMS) Phone Calls available to your therapist anytime you're in need. All information is treated with the utmost confidence and is used to improve our service for future patients.
